Access Denied

Establishing Meaningful Data Access for Algorithm Audits

Conference Paper Data Access Privacy-enhancing technologies

Decision-making algorithms are deployed in high-stakes domains, such as welfare services, hiring, or the justice system. Independent audits have previously revealed problematic and discriminatory behaviors, which can easily go undetected without proper oversight. However, auditors often face significant access barriers, and most algorithms remain unscrutinized due to lack of transparency. This study investigates how these access barriers affect the reliability of fairness audits, and explores solutions to establish meaningful access without infringing on individual privacy rights.
